The records from the city of Marseille constitute the oldest and currently the richest set of inventories in the entire DALME collection. The initial set was identified by Dan Smail in archival work dating from the 1990s and was substantially enlarged by Nathan Melson in 2011–12. Additional inventories were collected in 2018, including a set of inventories previously transcribed and published by Christine Barnel.

Highlights

The collection includes some of the earliest lay inventories from modern France and offers a relatively even sample covering nearly two centuries. It boasts two exceedingly rare inventories of Jewish men (1348 [incomplete] and 1405), as well as a substantial number of women (12). Other highlights include several inventories of apothecaries' shops and of very poor individuals. A register from 1420-22, kept by officials charged with distributing the assets of insolvent estates, includes the appraised values of the goods listed. Eight inventories feature households in the nearby town of Aubagne. Several inventories of churches and canonries round out the collection.
